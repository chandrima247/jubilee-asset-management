What Is a Unit Trust

What is a unit trust?

A unit trust pools investor money into one professionally managed portfolio, giving you diversification and expert oversight from the start.

Pooled Funds

Your money is invested alongside other investors in one fund.

Diversified

Your investment is spread across different assets, not one holding.

Expert Managed

Professional fund managers make the portfolio decisions for you.

Long-term Discipline

Built for more disciplined investing over time.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers for investors comparing the funds, assessing fit, and understanding the pre-application process.

What are the main public retail fund options here?+
For this landing page, the clearest retail fund choices are the Money Market Fund and the Balanced Fund. The Money Market Fund is positioned as the flagship retail option, while the Balanced Fund is the more growth-oriented alternative.
Why should the money market fund lead the retail story?+
The money market fund is the clearest starting point for most retail investors because it is built around liquidity, lower risk, and capital preservation. The Balanced Fund still matters, but it suits investors who can accept more market movement for growth potential.
What does each fund invest in?+
The Money Market Fund is designed for liquid, lower-risk income assets such as deposits, treasury bills, and other short-duration money market instruments. The Balanced Fund is different: it combines listed shares, debt, government securities, and money market instruments, so it is better understood as a mixed growth-and-income portfolio.
How liquid is the money market fund, and are returns guaranteed?+
The money market fund is positioned as a liquidity-focused option, and public summaries commonly cite about 3 business days for withdrawals. More importantly, returns should never be treated as guaranteed. Yields and prices can change over time, and past performance is not a promise of future results.
How does the application journey work before full onboarding?+
The public journey appears to begin with basic lead capture and then move into forms or assisted onboarding. The safest pre-application guidance is to keep your full name, mobile number, email address, location, chosen fund, contribution amount, and preferred contribution method ready, then refer to the official application form for the full list of supporting documents and declarations.
What disclosures should investors keep in mind?+
This unit-trust offer should be understood as a Kenya investment product regulated by the Capital Markets Authority. Withholding tax may apply on dividend and interest, and unit values or yields may rise or fall with market conditions. Performance should be monitored through official reporting materials rather than through any promise of fixed returns.
